Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer pseudonymous digital currency. "Peer-to-peer" (P2P) means that there is no central authority creating new money or clearing transactions. Instead, those tasks are managed collectively by the nodes in the network making it censorship resistant. "Pseudonymous" means that the real world identity of the parties of a transaction can be kept hidden from the public because sender and recipient are only identified by their bitcoin addresses. The bitcoin addresses and transaction however are fully public.
